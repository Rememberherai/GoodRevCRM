# GoodRev for Non-Profits — What It Does For You

## Two Products, One Platform

GoodRev for Non-Profits gives you two things that work together:

1. **A Digital Assistant** — a mobile-first AI that handles your paperwork through conversation. Text it a receipt photo, tell it to onboard a contractor, assign a job. It does the data entry so you don't have to.

2. **A Community Center Platform** — a web-based command center for managing everything: households, programs, grants, contractors, volunteers, community assets, and impact measurement.

The assistant is what you use on your phone, on the go. The platform is what you use at your desk. They share the same data.

---

## The Digital Assistant

### Accounts Payable — "Just Send It a Photo"

**The problem:** You're out buying supplies, at an event, in the field. Receipts pile up. QuickBooks data entry is a bottleneck.

**How it works:**
- Take a photo of a receipt or invoice
- Send it to the assistant via chat
- Add context: "This is for the youth garden program"
- Assistant reads the receipt and sends back: *"$47.23 from Home Depot on 3/18, Account: Supplies, Class: Youth Programs. Correct?"*
- You confirm
- Assistant files the receipt image in your cloud folder, creates the entry in QuickBooks, and attaches the receipt

That's it. Photo, confirm, done.

### Contractor Management — "Set Up John Smith"

**The problem:** Onboarding contractors means scope of work, W9, waivers, photo releases, policy acknowledgments. It's all manual and things fall through the cracks.

**How it works:**
- Tell the assistant: *"Set up John Smith as a new contractor"*
- It asks you for: start date, end date (or month-to-month), job description, key provisions, compensation
- It generates a scope of work and shows it to you for review
- You make edits until you're happy with it
- It asks which forms to send (W9, waiver, photo release, scope of work, policy docs — or all of them)
- You give it the contractor's phone number or email
- Documents go out for signature
- You get notified when everything is signed

### Job Assignment — "Assign This Job to Maria"

**The problem:** Assigning work to contractors is informal — texts, calls, no paper trail. Time tracking is on the honor system.

**How it works:**
- Tell the assistant: *"Assign a job to Maria Lopez"*
- Describe the job: what needs to be done, when to start, deadline, priority, service address
- Assistant checks if the job fits within Maria's scope of work
  - If it doesn't fit, it tells you and suggests a scope amendment
  - You can fix the scope, find another contractor, or send it anyway
- Maria gets notified and can accept or decline
- If she accepts, the job shows up on her phone with a Start/Pause/Stop clock
- When she's done, she marks it complete and adds notes
- You get a notification: *"Maria completed [job]. Total time: 3h 45m."*

**Built-in safety rails:**
- If the clock runs too long, the assistant asks: *"Is your clock still running?"*
- If a job isn't started by the desired date, the assistant reminds the contractor
- If no action after several days, the assistant asks you if you want to pull the job
- You can pull any job at any time

### Contractor's Side

Contractors can also talk to the assistant:
- *"What's on my calendar this week?"* — see accepted jobs
- *"How should I approach the week?"* — get a prioritized work plan based on deadlines

## The Community Center Platform

### Impact Measurement — Your Framework, Your Way

**The problem:** Funders want to know your impact. But different funders use different frameworks. You need flexibility.

The platform supports multiple impact frameworks:

- **Community Capitals Framework (CCF)** — 7 capitals: Financial, Human, Social, Built, Natural, Cultural, Political
- **7 Vital Conditions for Health** — 7 dimensions used by the Well Being In the Nation Network
- **Custom** — define your own dimensions with colors, icons, and descriptions

Everything you track — programs, contributions, assets, volunteer hours — gets tagged to your framework's dimensions. When a funder asks about impact, you have real data across every dimension.

### Households & People

**The problem:** You serve families, not isolated individuals. You need to know who lives together and see the full picture.

- Track households with addresses, members, and relationships
- Full history: when people join or leave a household, the record stays intact
- See each household's complete picture: programs, services, contributions, relationships
- Automatic mapping — addresses show up on your community map

### Programs & Attendance

**The problem:** Funders care about participation and consistency — not just enrollment, but actual attendance and hours.

- Create programs with schedules, locations, and capacity limits
- Enroll individuals or entire households
- **Batch attendance** — pull up a session, see everyone enrolled, check off who's present. 30 seconds.
- Track hours per participant ("dosage") — the metric funders actually ask for
- Waitlist management and waiver enforcement (no participation without a signed waiver)
- Program performance: total attendance, unique visitors, completion rates

### Grant Management

**The problem:** Grants are fragmented — finding opportunities in one place, tracking deadlines in another, writing proposals in a third, reporting in a fourth.

- **Discovery:** AI helps find grants that match your programs
- **Pipeline tracking:** Researching → Preparing → Submitted → Under Review → Awarded/Declined
- **Deadline management:** Automatic reminders for LOI deadlines, application deadlines, report deadlines
- **Grantor outreach:** Create and send multi-touch emails to program officers at funder organizations
- **Grant writing support:** AI pulls your real program data — attendance, impact metrics, demographics — directly into grant narratives
- **Compliance reporting:** Per-grant tracking of spend, unduplicated participants, hours delivered

### Contractor & Volunteer Management

**The problem:** You work with contractors and volunteers. Tracking their documents, hours, and work is scattered across spreadsheets, texts, and memory.

**Contractors:**
- Full directory with document status (W9, waivers, scope of work — what's signed, what's pending)
- Active jobs, completed jobs, total hours
- Invoicing support
- All managed through the Digital Assistant or the platform

**Volunteers:**
- Hour logging tied to specific programs
- Hours linked to the households and people they serve
- Dollar value calculation for grant reporting ($33.49/hr default, configurable)
- Recognition milestones at thresholds you set

### Community Assets & Facilities

**The problem:** You manage buildings, equipment, and spaces. You need to know what's available, what condition it's in, and when it's booked.

- Track every asset: buildings, land, equipment, vehicles, technology
- Condition ratings and responsibility assignments
- Facility booking with calendar view and double-booking prevention
- Everything shows on your community map

### Community Map

**The problem:** Where are your people? Where are your assets? What neighborhoods are you not reaching?

- Interactive map showing households, assets, programs, and partner organizations
- Toggle layers, filter by framework dimension or condition
- Click any marker to see details
- Addresses are automatically geocoded

### Relationships & Social Networks

**The problem:** Community building is about connections. You need to know who's connected and who's isolated.

- Track relationships between people (neighbor, mentor, caregiver, family, friend)
- Identify local influencers — people with the most connections
- Identify isolated individuals who need outreach

### Household Risk Index

**The problem:** Some families are falling through the cracks and you don't know until it's too late.

The system automatically scores each household's vulnerability based on:
- Not enrolled in any programs
- No social relationships recorded
- Open referrals that haven't been resolved
- No recent engagement
- Young children or single-adult household

High-risk households get flagged so your team can reach out before someone disappears.

### Referral Management

**The problem:** You refer families to partner organizations but lose track of whether they actually got help.

- Partner directory (organizations marked as referral partners)
- Status tracking: Submitted → Acknowledged → In Progress → Completed
- Automatic follow-up reminders at 7, 14, and 30 days
- Track outcomes — not just that you made the referral, but what happened

### Broadcast Messaging

**The problem:** You need to reach your community fast — schedule changes, weather closures, event reminders.

- Send via email, SMS, or both
- Filter recipients by program, household, or custom criteria
- Preview who will receive it before sending
- Full message history

### Automated Workflows

**The problem:** Your staff is stretched thin. Follow-ups slip through the cracks.

What happens automatically:
- New household enrolled → welcome message sent
- Program enrollment → confirmation sent, waiver prompted if needed
- Attendance drops off → staff alerted
- Referral not progressing → follow-up reminders at 7/14/30 days
- Grant deadline approaching → reminder to assigned staff
- Job assigned to contractor → notification sent
- Job not started by deadline → inaction warning
- High risk score detected → case manager alerted

Scheduled checks run automatically:
- **Nightly:** risk score recalculation, overdue referral checks
- **Weekly:** grant deadline review
- **Monthly:** grant compliance checks

### Dashboard & Reporting

**The problem:** When the board asks "how are we doing?" or a funder asks for numbers, you need answers fast.

**Dashboard (your daily view):**
- Impact radar chart across all framework dimensions
- Key metrics: households, programs, hours, contributions, total attendance, unique visitors
- Program performance cards
- At-risk household alerts
- % of population impacted

**Reports include:**
- Impact framework summary (all dimensions with scores)
- Program performance (enrollment, dosage, completion, unique visitors)
- Unduplicated participant counts (the standard funders require)
- Volunteer impact (hours, dollar value, FTE equivalent)
- Grant compliance (per-grant spend, participants, hours)
- Referral outcomes by partner
- Contractor hours by job and date range
- Household demographics and geographic spread
- Relationship network density and key influencers
- % of population/households impacted
- Performance to goals

## The Bottom Line

This is two tools working together:

**On your phone:** An assistant that handles the administrative work you hate — receipts, contractor paperwork, job assignments, reminders. Talk to it like a person. It does the data entry.

**At your desk:** A platform that shows you the full picture — who you're serving, what programs are working, where your grants stand, who's at risk, and what impact you're making across every dimension that matters to your funders.

Everything connects. A household enrolls in a program, attends sessions, receives a referral, gets volunteer support, a contractor completes a job at the facility — and all of it maps back to your impact framework so you can show the full picture of what your center does for the community.
